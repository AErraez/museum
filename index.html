Explore the MET: Discover your History

Search and observe artifacts from all around the world and different points in time.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art (MET) is one of the world's most renowned art museums, located in the Manhattan district of New York City, United States. The museum's collection includes more than two million works of art from around the world.

We invite you to explore this portal, where you can discover the wide variety of artistic pieces housed in the museum. We hope you enjoy this experience and feel inspired to visit the museum on one of your future trips.

Tips and Tricks

Choose an object type and compare how it differs between two countries using the map. For example, the design and materials of weapons and instruments from England vary greatly from those of Japan.

Select an object type and country, then use the date filters to see how the objects evolved over time. For instance, in the U.S. from 1700 to 1800, realism predominated, while from 1800 to 1900, paintings leaned more towards impressionism.

Use the range slider to see how the distribution of objects changes over time. For example, from 1000 to 1500, most objects were sculptures, whereas from 1500 onwards, ceramic and glass objects became more prevalent.
